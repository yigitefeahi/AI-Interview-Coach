# Turkish Company Interview Context (Overview)

Large Turkish employers often combine global engineering expectations with local operational realities: peak campaign traffic in e-commerce, telecom-scale reliability, or defense/aerospace quality discipline. Candidates should adapt answers to sector context while keeping STAR structure, personal ownership, and measurable results.

Defense and aerospace (Aselsan, TUSAŞ, Havelsan): emphasize verification, safety, documentation, integration, and reliability. E-commerce and delivery (Trendyol, Hepsiburada, Getir): emphasize scale, performance, experimentation, and operational metrics. Telecom (Turkcell, Türk Telekom): emphasize availability, transformation, customer impact, and enterprise-grade rollout.

Banking and fintech (Garanti BBVA, Akbank, Papara): emphasize transaction correctness, security, fraud awareness, audit readiness, and customer trust. Mobile gaming (Peak Games, Dream Games): emphasize gamecraft, live ops, device performance, and player metrics. Aviation (Pegasus): emphasize operational efficiency, peak travel readiness, booking reliability, and cost-aware delivery.

Defense and aerospace (Aselsan, TUSAŞ, Havelsan): emphasize verification, traceability, safety culture, integration rigor, and documentation—not speed alone. Telecom (Turkcell, Türk Telekom): emphasize peak availability, subscriber metrics, legacy migration risk, and enterprise SLA discipline. E-commerce and quick commerce (Trendyol, Hepsiburada, Getir): emphasize campaign peaks, oversell prevention, delivery SLA, and conversion metrics.

Interviewers frequently accept Turkish or English depending on the team, but structured communication and technical clarity matter in both languages. Strong candidates explain constraints openly, avoid exaggeration, and describe how they validated results in production or pilot environments.
