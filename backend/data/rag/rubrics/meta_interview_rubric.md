# Meta Interview Rubric Signals

Meta-style loops reward impact at scale, execution speed, and crisp communication. Strong candidates explain how their work moved a product metric, improved reliability, or unlocked a team. They show comfort with ambiguity, rapid iteration, and cross-functional influence.

Technical depth still matters, especially for backend, infra, and ML roles, but answers should connect engineering choices to user or business impact quickly. Strong behavioral stories include conflict resolution, feedback loops, and recovering from a failed launch with learning.

Weak Meta answers are overly academic, slow to state the result, or missing scale context. Another anti-pattern is describing feature work without explaining why it mattered or how success was measured.

Coaching should emphasize: headline impact first, then mechanism, then tradeoffs. Encourage candidates to mention experimentation, rollout, monitoring, or iteration when relevant.
