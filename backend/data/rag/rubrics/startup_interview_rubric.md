# Startup Interview Rubric Signals

Startup loops prioritize versatility, speed, ownership, and practical judgment with limited resources. Strong candidates show they can scope ruthlessly, ship incrementally, and measure whether the bet worked. They explain what they personally built, unblocked, or decided under uncertainty.

Useful signals include wearing multiple hats, talking to users, making reversible decisions, and fixing production issues quickly. Financial and resource awareness matters: why this feature now, what was intentionally not built, and how success was validated cheaply.

Weak startup answers sound like big-company process theater without adaptability. Red flags include over-engineering, no customer contact, and inability to describe tradeoffs between speed and quality.

Coaching should help candidates show bias for action, learning loops, and direct impact with limited headcount.
