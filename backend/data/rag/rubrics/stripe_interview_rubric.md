# Stripe Interview Rubric Signals

Stripe loops reward precision, user empathy, operational quality, and clear writing. Strong answers explain customer pain, edge cases, failure modes, and long-term maintainability. API and product answers should show careful naming, validation, backward compatibility, and observability.

Candidates should demonstrate principled tradeoffs: why a simpler design was chosen, how risk was reduced, and how quality was verified before launch. For incident stories, strong answers include detection, mitigation, customer communication, and permanent prevention.

Weak Stripe answers hand-wave edge cases, ignore developer experience, or propose clever but fragile designs. Behavioral anti-patterns include vague impact and missing detail on how mistakes were corrected.

Coaching tips: push for explicit edge cases, rollback plans, monitoring, and written communication examples when the role is customer-facing or platform-focused.
