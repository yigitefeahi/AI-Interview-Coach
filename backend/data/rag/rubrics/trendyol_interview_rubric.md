# Trendyol Interview Rubric Signals

Trendyol-style interviews reward e-commerce scale thinking, execution speed, performance optimization, and measurable business impact. Strong candidates show they can build and operate systems under peak traffic events such as campaigns, holidays, and marketplace growth. Ownership, pragmatism, and data-informed iteration are core signals.

Technical interviews often cover marketplace architecture, search and recommendation adjacency, order and payment flows, inventory consistency, caching, and observability. Strong answers include peak-load assumptions, idempotency, failure isolation, and cost-aware design. Weak answers ignore race conditions, duplicate orders, or seller/buyer edge cases.

Behavioral loops look for bias for action, collaboration under pressure, and learning from failed experiments. Strong examples quantify conversion, GMV support, latency reduction, defect rate, or operational efficiency gains.

Red flags: no production war stories, no metrics, over-abstract architecture without business context, or inability to explain tradeoffs between speed and correctness during peak events.

Coaching tips: frame answers with customer and merchant impact, then system design, then validation during load tests or canary releases. Mention campaign readiness and incident playbooks when relevant.

Common themes: high QPS services, catalog/search performance, fulfillment coordination, fraud and abuse edges, and rapid experimentation with safe rollout.
