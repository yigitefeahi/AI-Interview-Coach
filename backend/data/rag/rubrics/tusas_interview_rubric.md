# TUSAŞ Interview Rubric Signals

TUSAŞ (Turkish Aerospace Industries) loops expect aerospace-grade engineering discipline, safety culture, documentation quality, and precision in technical reasoning. Strong candidates connect design choices to verification, risk reduction, and long-term maintainability. Answers should show respect for process without hiding personal ownership.

Technical depth matters in structures, avionics, software, manufacturing support, or systems engineering depending on the role. Strong answers include assumptions, validation methods, interface control, and failure analysis. Weak answers jump to solutions without requirements or ignore test and certification thinking.

Behavioral signals include cross-disciplinary collaboration, accountability, learning from mistakes, and calm execution under schedule pressure. Strong examples use STAR with clear metrics: schedule recovery, defect reduction, test pass rate, or process improvement.

Red flags: hand-waving safety implications, no mention of verification, blaming other departments, or inability to explain tradeoffs between weight, cost, performance, and reliability.

Coaching tips: push candidates to mention verification plans, interface definitions, risk registers, and post-incident learning. Encourage precise language and structured communication suitable for engineering review boards.

Typical themes: design verification, quality escapes, integration testing, requirement changes, and coordinating with multiple engineering teams.
