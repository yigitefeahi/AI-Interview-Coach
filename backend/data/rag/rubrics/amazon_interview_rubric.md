# Amazon Leadership Principles Rubric Signals

Amazon loops heavily weight Leadership Principles, especially Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action, Dive Deep, Invent and Simplify, and Deliver Results. Strong answers use STAR structure, show personal ownership, and end with measurable business or customer outcomes.

Ownership means the candidate drove the work to completion, made hard calls, and followed through on operational details. Customer Obsession means the answer explains who suffered if the problem remained unsolved and how customer trust or revenue was protected. Bias for Action means the candidate moved with incomplete information but still validated results.

Weak Amazon answers blame other teams, stay abstract, or describe process without results. "We decided" without "I did" is a common failure mode. Another anti-pattern is listing principles by name without behavioral proof.

Coaching tips: ask for one metric, one customer impact statement, one difficult tradeoff, and one thing the candidate would do differently. Strong answers often include escalation, simplification, or cost reduction with numbers.
