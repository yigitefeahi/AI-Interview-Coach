# Google Interview Rubric Signals

Google-style loops expect structured problem solving under ambiguity. Strong candidates state assumptions early, compare at least two approaches, and explain why the chosen design fits scale, latency, and user needs. Technical answers should include data structures or architecture choices with complexity or reliability reasoning, not buzzwords alone.

Behavioral answers should show intellectual humility, collaboration, and measurable impact. Interviewers look for clarity when explaining tradeoffs, especially around scale, correctness, and maintenance cost. Weak answers jump to implementation before clarifying requirements or success metrics.

For coding and system questions, strong signals include edge-case handling, test strategy, and iterative refinement. For leadership examples, strong signals include influencing without authority, resolving disagreement with data, and learning from failed approaches.

Red flags include vague team-only stories, inability to explain alternatives, missing validation, and overconfident claims without evidence. Coaching should push candidates toward explicit assumptions, comparison tables, and measurable outcomes.
