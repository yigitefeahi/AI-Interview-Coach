# Turkcell Interview Rubric Signals

Turkcell-style interviews blend telecom-scale engineering with digital product delivery. Strong candidates show customer and revenue impact, data-informed decisions, agile execution, and comfort with high-availability systems. Mobile, network, billing, digital channels, and platform teams each emphasize different depth, but clarity and ownership remain constant.

Technical loops may cover distributed systems, APIs, data platforms, mobile backends, cloud migration, or network-adjacent software. Strong answers include scale assumptions, monitoring, rollout strategy, and incident response. Weak answers ignore peak traffic, failover, or customer-visible failure modes.

Product and business alignment matter: experiments, KPI movement, and cross-team delivery are valued. Strong behavioral answers show influence across product, engineering, and operations with measurable outcomes such as churn reduction, conversion lift, latency improvement, or cost savings.

Red flags: no metrics, no mention of production responsibility, over-engineering without business framing, or inability to explain prioritization under resource constraints.

Coaching tips: lead with user or business impact, then technical mechanism, then tradeoffs. Mention A/B tests, dashboards, SLIs, and post-launch iteration when relevant.

Common themes: high-traffic services, digital transformation, platform modernization, customer experience, and fast iteration with operational safety.
