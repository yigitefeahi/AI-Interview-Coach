# Türk Telekom Interview Rubric Signals

Türk Telekom interviews often emphasize large-enterprise delivery, network and infrastructure reliability, transformation programs, and stakeholder management. Strong candidates show they can operate in complex organizations while still driving measurable outcomes. Technical roles require depth in platforms, integration, OSS/BSS adjacency, cloud, or enterprise software depending on the team.

Strong technical answers explain non-functional requirements first: availability, recovery time, security, capacity, and operational cost. Candidates should describe how changes are rolled out safely in environments where downtime has broad customer impact. Weak answers focus on tools without architecture or operational planning.

Behavioral answers should demonstrate persistence on long-running programs, communication with business stakeholders, and ownership across handoffs between teams or vendors. Strong STAR examples include migration milestones, incident reduction, SLA improvement, or successful rollout of a enterprise-wide capability.

Red flags: ignoring legacy constraints, no rollback plan, vague impact statements, or inability to explain how work aligned with business priorities.

Coaching tips: use enterprise language carefully—stability, integration, governance, phased rollout—while keeping personal contribution explicit. Mention monitoring, change management, and customer impact for major systems.

Typical themes: network/platform modernization, B2B service reliability, transformation projects, vendor integration, and cross-department coordination.
