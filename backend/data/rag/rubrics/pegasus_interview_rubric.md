# Pegasus Interview Rubric Signals

Pegasus-style interviews combine airline operational reality with digital product and engineering delivery. Strong candidates show awareness of cost efficiency, schedule reliability, customer experience, and peak travel demand without sacrificing safety and compliance expectations.

Technical roles may touch booking and digital channels, pricing/revenue systems adjacency, mobile apps, integration with ops systems, data platforms, or internal tooling. Strong answers explain availability during campaign or holiday peaks, incident handling affecting passengers, and phased rollout for customer-visible changes. Weak answers ignore operational consequences of downtime or inconsistent inventory/seat data.

Behavioral interviews look for accountability, cross-team coordination (ops, product, IT, vendors), and practical problem solving under time pressure.

Red flags: no customer-impact framing, no peak-load thinking, vague ownership, or inability to explain recovery after a production issue affecting travelers.

Coaching tips: mention booking conversion, check-in reliability, mobile app stability, support load reduction, or operational cost savings with evidence.

Common themes: peak season readiness, payment and booking flows, disruption handling, partner integration, and cost-aware engineering.
