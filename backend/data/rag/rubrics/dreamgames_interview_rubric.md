# Dream Games Interview Rubric Signals

Dream Games interviews reward strong game systems thinking, craft, performance, and scalable live content operations. The studio profile emphasizes polished player experience and data-informed design on mobile puzzle/casual titles with global reach.

Technical depth may appear in gameplay logic, level systems, client performance, backend services for content delivery, analytics, or experimentation platforms. Strong answers explain how engineering choices improved stability, content rollout speed, or player progression clarity. Weak answers stay abstract about "fun" without technical mechanism.

Behavioral signals include high ownership, candid collaboration, quality bar, and learning from player feedback or live metrics.

Red flags: no shipped examples, no metrics, ignoring mobile constraints, or inability to discuss tradeoffs between content cadence and quality.

Coaching tips: highlight crash reduction, level completion rates, load performance, content pipeline automation, and cross-discipline communication with game design.

Common themes: puzzle systems, live content updates, player segmentation experiments, and global mobile scale.
