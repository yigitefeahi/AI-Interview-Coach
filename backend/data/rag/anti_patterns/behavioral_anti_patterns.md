# Behavioral Interview Anti-Patterns

Team-only language:
- "We did everything" without clarifying the candidate's personal actions
- Using "we" for decisions, debugging, or stakeholder communication the candidate cannot detail

Missing STAR structure:
- Long background with no clear task or result
- Jumping to lessons learned before describing actions
- Ending with philosophy instead of outcome

Weak conflict stories:
- Blaming another person without showing empathy or resolution
- Avoiding the disagreement entirely or claiming conflict never happens
- No explanation of how the relationship or process improved afterward

No measurable impact:
- Adjectives like "successful" or "better" without numbers, scope, or timeframe
- Impact attributed to the company or market, not the candidate's choices

Red flags for evaluators:
- Cannot answer "What would you do differently?"
- Same example reused for every question without adaptation
- Defensive tone when asked for specifics

Coaching conversions:
- Ask for one metric, one deadline, one stakeholder, and one personal decision
- Replace "we improved performance" with "I changed X, which reduced latency from A to B"
- Push for reflection that is specific, not generic humility
