# CV and Seniority Interview Signals

Junior candidate signals:
- Learning speed, fundamentals, debugging process, and collaboration
- Expect clearer guidance needs but strong curiosity and validation habits
- Questions can focus on small scoped ownership, tests, and communication with mentors

Mid-level candidate signals:
- End-to-end delivery on features, reliable execution, and growing technical judgment
- Expect ownership within a component or service, not full system strategy alone
- Probe for tradeoffs, code review quality, and incident follow-through

Senior candidate signals:
- Cross-team influence, architecture judgment, mentoring, and risk management
- Expect ambiguity handling, metric-driven decisions, and operational maturity
- Ask about scaling paths, org constraints, and long-term maintainability

Staff-plus signals:
- Multi-team impact, technical direction, and aligning engineering with business strategy
- Expect system-level thinking, roadmap influence, and clear communication upward and outward

CV red flags to probe gently in interview:
- Buzzword stacks without outcomes
- No measurable impact statements
- Very short tenures without explanation
- Leadership titles without examples of influence

CV strong signals to explore:
- Metrics in bullet points (% improvement, latency, revenue, cost, reliability)
- Clear ownership verbs: designed, led, migrated, reduced, launched, automated
- Evidence of production responsibility: on-call, incidents, monitoring, rollout

Coaching use: align question difficulty and rubric weights with inferred seniority, not only job title.
