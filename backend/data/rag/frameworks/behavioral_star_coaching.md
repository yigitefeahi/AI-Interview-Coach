# STAR Behavioral Framework With Coaching Prompts

STAR structure:
- Situation: one or two sentences of context, not a long history
- Task: what the candidate was responsible for, distinct from the team goal
- Action: specific steps the candidate took, in order, using "I"
- Result: measurable outcome, business or user impact, and learning

Coaching prompts when Situation is too long:
- "What was the problem in one sentence?"
- "Why did this matter to the business or user?"

Coaching prompts when Action is weak:
- "What did you personally do first?"
- "What alternative did you reject and why?"

Coaching prompts when Result is missing:
- "What metric moved?"
- "What happened within 30 days?"
- "What would have happened if you did nothing?"

Senior candidate extension — add Tradeoff and Reflection:
- Tradeoff: speed vs quality, build vs buy, short-term fix vs long-term architecture
- Reflection: what you would repeat, what you would change, what process was added afterward

Red flags: passive voice, no numbers, no personal ownership, no validation of success.

Strong STAR answers often include one stakeholder, one metric, one deadline, and one preventive follow-up.
